What is 5 Brain Abilities Assessment?

The 5 Brain Abilities Assessment is an important process to understand your child's brain abilities (its strengths and weaknesses) at an early stage as early stage development will help your child to grow up more confident in their learning abilities. Children's brain development shall be well-balanced in order to realize their full potential. Children’s brain abilities strengths and weaknesses are hardly detectable at young age.These 5 brain abilities assessment are mean for educational purposes for parents to take early initiatives to help their child and not from medical perspective.

When is the best time to start assessing my kids' brain development?

The best time to develop your child's brain is before they turn 8. At birth, the brain weighs around 360 grams and grows to about 1270 grams by age 7. As the child grows, the brain continues to develop until it reaches its full adult size of 1300 to 1400 grams. Therefore, the ideal time to assess a child's brain abilities is between the ages of 4 and 8 when their brains are actively developing.

Why should I understand my child's brain profile?

Understanding your children’s 5 brain abilities early will enable earlier planning with longer time for improvement thus reducing parental stress as they entering primary education. Most brain abilities weaknesses can be improved over time. We recommend parents to start on their children’s brain strengths then work on improving the weaknesses progressively. This way will improve your child’s confidence without being affected from their weaknesses.

How often should I assess the progress of my child's brain development?

We suggest assessing your child's brain development once a year to track their progress.

What are the 5 major areas of brain abilities?

The 5 major areas of brain abilities are Attention, Cognitive Visual, Memory, Logic Reasoning, and Processing Speed. Examples of these 5 brain abilities:

Attention

 Able to focus on tasks for an extended period of timeSit through classes and exams until break time or completionAble to absorb information and learn through listeningRemain attentive even with distractions

Cognitive Visual

 See visuals exactly to copy or uses the information correctlyUnderstand concepts or texts through visualisation of informationAbility to uncover the co-relationships of charts and diagramsFlexibility in maneuvering visuals in the mindAble to observe patterns for abstract thinking

Memory

 Able to remember and retrieve information learnt in classAble to hold information mentally while using itUses auditory and visual memory to enhance learning

Logical Reasoning

 Apply reasoning and deduction to drive solutionsAbility to think and solve spatial problems that requires high levelsof conceptual abilitiesConfident and independent problem-solver

Brain Speed

 The speed at which the brain does math calculations andprocess informationAbility to complete homework quickly and accuratelyTendency to work hard now to free up time for enjoyable activities

Stronger 5 brain abilities are key to success in children’s life including education. When children have stronger 5 brain abilities, they can easily understand, remember and solve problems from what they’ve learned. This makes them more confident, happy, and eager to learn more. Their curiosities grows as they enjoy new discoveries. With each success and failure, their self-esteem progressively build up, encouraging them to tackle challenges rather than avoid them. This cycle of achievement not only leads to higher accomplishments but also further stimulate and develop their brains.
Education results come from the learning process, and stronger 5 brain abilities make this process smoother and more enjoyable for the child, leading to better and encouraging educational results without negative stress. Learning becomes fun with happier children, and success naturally follows!
