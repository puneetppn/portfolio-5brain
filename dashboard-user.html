Assessment Steps and Instructions

Please read the "Guidelines" and the "Instructions Guidebook" before you take the assessment to ensure a smooth assessment.

Guidelines

2. It takes less than an hour to complete the assessment.

1. 5 Brain Abilities assessment assesses the 5 brain abilities of

  • Attention
  • Cognitive Visual
  • Memory
  • Logical Reasoning
  • Brain Speed

3. WHAT TO DO BEFORE THE ASSESSMENT

  • Make sure you have read the Guidelines.
  • Read and go through the Instructions Guidebook ("download" below) together with the child to understand the flow of the assessment.
  • Only begin the assessment when the child is ready.
  • Make sure all items (as stated in point 4) are ready.
  • Make sure to update the child's current age under "Profile" tabs (on the top right corner) for returning user.

4. MAKE SURE YOU HAVE

  • An adult present to provide assistance needed by the child.
  • A working keyboard and mouse/trackpad.
  • Speaker at an optimal volume for the child.
  • A maximum of 1-hour time.
  • A piece of paper and pencil for working calculations only for Age 10-12 - Pattern Sequencing component.
  • A quiet environment with no noise disruption.
  • Environment with adequate lighting.
  • h. Good internet connection and smooth web browser.

5. WHAT YOU DO NOT NEED and SHOULD NOT DO

  • Do not take breaks after starting the assessment.
  • Avoid giving hints or answers to the child.
  • Do not interfere with the child's answer selection.
  • Do not write answers on papers or use calculators.
  • All answers must be typed in or selected from the mouse.

6. WHAT TEST ADMINISTRATOR SHOULD DO

  • Make sure the child is in their best condition and mood for the assessment.
  • Set a positive mindset for the child.
  • Constantly give encouragements to the child to complete all assessment components.
  • Test administrator may help the child to select or key in answers if they are unable to use the mouse or keyboard independently.
  • Do gently remind or pull their attention back to complete the assessment in 1 sitting.
  • Give the child their freedom to select answer based on their choice.
