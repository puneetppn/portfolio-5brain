5 Brain Abilities?

We all possess 5 major brain abilities. The best time to nurture healthy brain abilities is during the early childhood days.

Attention

Attention is the ability to focus and concentrate on a task or situation at hand. It is the primary abilities we utilise when learning. Attention is a prerequisite skill in order for effective learning to take place.

More

Cognitive Visual

Cognitive Visual is the ability to form mental images or pictures from the information we received from our external environment. We utilise our cognitive visual ability to break down the massive amount of information around us into recognisable patterns and points for us to better interpret, digest and assimilate with it.

More

Memory

Memory is the ability to remember and store information in our brain to be retrieve later on when in need. The short-term memory or also known as working memory stores information temporarily in our brain before it is being transferred to our long-term memory for permanent storage through sufficient workings. Memory is an important ability of retaining information over time for the purpose of influencing future actions.

More

Logical Reasoning

Logical Thinking is the ability of strategizing solutions and planning the steps to solve complex problems we face in our everyday life. Logical thinking enables us to make sense of our surroundings and make accurate and appropriate decision to the options we have. It is a crucial skill that a child's needs not only in the subject matter of arithmetic, but for all areas of life.

More

Processing Speed

Processing Speed is the ability of the pace at which information is being process. Processing speed is the product of the latter 5 brain abilities.
